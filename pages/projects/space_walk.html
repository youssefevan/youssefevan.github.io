// Space Walk - Fall 2022

  • Role: Solo Developer
  • Engine: Godot
  • Project Type: Game Jam
  • Platform: Itch.io


Space Walk is a game I created during the Fall 2022 UNCC Game Jam.

Space Walk was my first-ever game jam entry and was completed in under 2 days. Since I was new to game jams, I decided to focus on a very simple gameplay system to avoid extensive bug fixing. While my inexperience at the time showed through the rudimentary UI and menus, I'm proud of what I was able to accomplish.

In Space Walk, the player launches an astronaut around planets and their gravitational pulls. The astronaut must orbit each planet in a level in order to complete the mission. The player has three charges to launch the astronaut, which recharge when the astronaut enters a planet's field of gravity. After orbiting each planet, the player must guide the astronaut to the space station.

When designing the game, I wanted the player to be able to make intentional, calculated decisions while sometimes including quick, improvisational choices in reaction to the physics system. There are two design choices that contribute to these feelings: momentum and limited charges.

The astronaut's momentum is taken into account when the player launches them in a direction. For instance, when inside a gravity field, the astronaut will get launched further if the player aims in the same direction that the astronaut is orbiting. Having limited launch charges forces the player to calculate when and where to launch the astronaut. If the player launches the astronaut at the wrong time, in the wrong direction, or with the wrong amount of power, they can get stuck inside of a gravity field. These two mechanics work together to create a push-and-pull effect between the player's intentions and reactions.
